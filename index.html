Any surface can be a floor, wall, or ceiling, and therefore astronauts can sleep anywhere. But, weak gravity means that the astronauts may gradually float away while sleeping. To keep that from happening, most astronauts on the ISS sleep by restraining their bodies in small sleeping compartments or sleeping bags.

Space travellers go through a range of often unpleasant feelings: they feel dizzy, sleepy and weak, suffer from loss of appetite and stomach upsets and lose their sense of time and space. Fortunately, after few days, their bodies adapt to weightlessness and astronauts start to feel both calm and euphoric...
